Monday · Gym

Upper A

Push · Shoulder stability · Wrist prep
Phase 1, Wks 1–2: 60–65% of max. Form over weight. Don't go to failure.
Warm-Up · 10 min
Cat-Cow with breath
10 reps
Spine awakening. Inhale to arch, exhale to round. Link to your morning yoga flow.
Thoracic rotation on all fours
8 each side
Opens upper back for overhead pressing. Hand behind head, rotate elbow toward ceiling.
Wall slides
10 slow reps
Stand against wall, arms in goalpost shape, slide up overhead. Activates lower traps and serratus — essential for handstand.
Wrist circles + wrist CARs
60 sec
Always do this before any loading. Slow controlled circles both directions, then controlled articular rotations.
Shoulder CARs
5 each direction
Full shoulder joint prep. Move as slowly as possible through maximum range in both directions.
Main Work
Yoga Finish · 10 min
Hollow body hold
3 × 20–30 sec
Ribs down, lower back pressed to floor, arms overhead, legs just off ground. This IS the handstand body position. The most important drill in this programme.
Pike compression hold
3 × 20 sec
Seated, legs straight, actively pull yourself into a forward fold using your hip flexors — not just gravity. This is active hamstring work, not passive stretching.
Prone cobra / Sphinx
2 × 30 sec
Lie face down, press up onto forearms or hands. Shoulder extension and hip flexor counter-stretch after all that pressing.
Friday · Gym

Upper B

Pull · Scapular control · Handstand drills
Phase 1: Reconnect. Quality over quantity. Don't grind to failure.
Warm-Up · 10 min
Dead hang (passive)
3 × 20–30 sec
Hang from the bar and let everything go. Decompresses the spine and tractions the shoulder. Do this every single session.
Scapular pull-ups
3 × 8
No elbow bend at all — just depress and retract your shoulder blades. Isolates the exact movement that holds you up in a handstand.
Band pull-aparts
15 reps
Hold band at shoulder height, pull apart to a T shape. Activates rear delts and mid-traps.
Downward dog walk-outs + pedal
5 reps
Walk hands out from standing into down dog, pedal the heels. Your yoga warm-up in gym context.
Pull-Up Progression 🎯
Grease the Groove Protocol
5 sets · Stop 2 reps short · Rest 90s
If your max is 6, do 4 per set. When all 5 sets feel easy, add 1 rep across all sets next week. Week 1 target: ~4 reps/set. This gives you 20 quality reps vs grinding out 2 ugly sets.
Slow Eccentric Negative Pull-Up
3 × 4–5 reps · 5 sec descent
Jump or step to get chin over bar, then lower as slowly as possible — aim for a full 5 seconds. Builds the strength exactly where you fail on rep 7 and 8.

Handstand Drill Block · 10–15 min
Wall handstand hold (chest to wall)
3 × 20–30 sec
Chest facing wall forces proper hollow body alignment. Safer and more effective than back to wall. Build time here before going freestanding.
Handstand kick-up practice
5–8 attempts
Back to wall. One leg at a time, strong hollow body, ribs down. Each attempt is a rep — volume of practice matters more than duration of hold.
Pike walk-ins (feet up wall)
5 reps
From downward dog, walk feet up the wall until hips stack over shoulders. Builds compression strength and comfort being inverted.
Tuck compression holds
3 × 15 sec
Seated, pull knees to chest with a straight back. Direct straddle handstand prep — the hip flexor compression that eventually lets you press up.
Wednesday · Gym

Lower A

Squat · Hip hinge · Compression strength
Phase 1: Full range of motion. Let the hip flexor stretch happen in every rep.
Warm-Up · 10 min
Hip 90-90 with active holds
5 each side
Sit with both legs at 90°, rotate between sides. Hold each position actively — squeeze the hip down rather than just collapsing into it. Best hip opener in this programme.
Lizard lunge with rotation
5 each side
Step foot outside hand, lower back knee. Add thoracic rotation — reach top hand to ceiling. Deep hip flexor and groin opening. This is your low lunge, loaded.
Leg swings
10 each direction
Hold wall for balance. Forward-back then lateral. Let momentum do the work — don't force range.
Glute bridge activation
15 reps
Wakes up glutes before squatting. Your hip flexors tend to dominate — this corrects that pattern before you load.
Main Work
Compression Block · 10 min
L-sit hold
3 × 10–20 sec
Use parallel bars or floor blocks. Legs straight, hips at 90°, press down through hands. THE compression exercise — direct training for Crow, Firefly, handstand.
Lying leg raise (slow)
3 × 12
Lower back stays pressed to floor the entire time. Lower abs and hip flexors — the muscles that hold your legs up in a handstand.
Seated forward fold — active pulling
3 × 20 sec
Hands on feet or shins, actively pull yourself down using your hip flexors rather than just folding. Active flexibility change is faster than passive.

Tuesday · Home · Mat

Yoga + Drills A

Hip flexors · Hamstrings · 45 min
No class needed — this IS the session. Work slowly. Breathe into resistance.
Part 1 — Hip Flexor Release & Strengthening · 15 min
PNF Low Lunge (contract-relax)
5 reps × 10 sec each side
In a low lunge, press your front foot into the floor for 10 seconds (isometric — don't actually move), then actively relax deeper. PNF is the most effective method for real hip flexor change — more than passive stretching.
Psoas March — standing
3 × 12 each side
Standing, drive one knee up against light resistance (your own hand or a band). You need length AND strength in the hip flexor — passive stretching alone won't fix tightness long-term.
Crescent lunge — lift back knee, hold 5 sec
5 reps each side
Start in low lunge with back knee down, then lift the back knee off the floor and hold for 5 seconds. Loads the hip flexor stretch while demanding active control.
Reclined hero pose (Supta Virasana)
2 min hold
Deep passive hip flexor and quad release. Use a bolster or folded blanket under your back if knees complain. Don't force this — it will come over weeks.
Couch stretch against wall
90 sec each side
Back shin up the wall, front foot forward in a lunge shape. One of the best psoas and rectus femoris openers. Hold passively, then add a gentle posterior pelvic tilt.
Part 2 — Hamstring Lengthening · 15 min
Active straight-leg raise
10 each side
Lying down, actively lift one straight leg as high as you can without the other leg or your lower back shifting. Tests and builds active hamstring flexibility — more useful than static holds.
Standing forward fold — slowly straighten knees
5 reps × 5 breaths
Start with bent knees, hang heavy, then gradually straighten a little more each exhale. Teaches the nervous system to release the hamstring rather than bracing against the stretch.
Strap-assisted passive hold
2 × 90 sec each side
Lying on back, strap around one foot, leg toward ceiling. Completely relax into it and breathe. Long passive holds are where genuine length changes actually happen.
Seated straddle — walk hands out
2 min hold
Legs wide, slowly walk hands forward keeping spine long. Adductor and hamstring combined. Let gravity work. Unlocks seated compression poses over time.
Pyramid pose (Parsvottanasana) with blocks
90 sec each side
Hands on blocks, front leg straight, fold forward over it. Very specific hamstring stretch — particularly effective if you carry tension in hips and lower back.
Thursday · Home · Mat

Yoga + Drills B

Inversion prep · Compression · 30–45 min
This is skill training, not stretching. Keep it focused and intentional.
Inversion & Compression Prep
Crow pose (Bakasana)
5–8 attempts × 10–20 sec
Knees on upper arms (not in armpits), round the back, look forward. Core compression, wrist load, balance. Your current arm balance to build — more attempts = faster progress.
Tripod or supported headstand
2–3 × 20–30 sec
Tripod: hands and crown of head form a triangle. Use the wall if needed. Focus on stacking hips over shoulders. Build hold time steadily.
Straddle compression — flat back lean
3 × 20 sec
Legs wide, lean torso forward with a completely flat back. Builds hip flexor strength for straddle handstand entry — not just flexibility.
Hollow body rock
3 × 20 sec
Ribs down, lower back to floor, arms overhead, legs just off ground. Rock gently. Same body shape as a good handstand — this is where gym and yoga connect.
Handstand kick-up practice against wall
5–8 attempts
Keep it playful. Each attempt is a rep. Focus on alignment, not duration. The handstand is a skill and skills need volume.
Child's pose + shoulder stretch
2 min
Cool down. Thread one arm under for a shoulder stretch each side. Passive wrist and shoulder release.
