Monday · Gym

Upper A

Push · Shoulder stability · Wrist prep
Phase 1, Weeks 1–2: 60–65% of max. Focus on form. Don't go to failure.
Warm-Up · 10 min
Cat-Cow with breath
10 reps
Spine awakening — link to your morning yoga flow. Inhale arch, exhale round.
Thoracic rotation on all fours
8 each side
Opens upper back for overhead pressing. Hand behind head, rotate elbow to ceiling.
Wall slides
10 slow reps
Activates lower traps and serratus — essential for handstand. Stand against wall, arms in goalpost, slide up.
Wrist circles + wrist CARs
60 sec
Always do this before any loading. Slow controlled circles in both directions.
Shoulder CARs
5 each direction
Controlled Articular Rotations — full shoulder joint prep. Slow, maximum range circles.
Main Work
Yoga Finish · 10 min
Hollow body hold
3 × 20–30 sec
Core compression — the body position that makes handstands work. Ribs down, lower back pressed to floor, arms overhead or by sides.
Pike compression hold
3 × 20 sec
Seated, legs straight, fold forward actively. Pull yourself down with hip flexors — not just gravity. This is active hamstring work.
Prone cobra / Sphinx
2 × 30 sec hold
Shoulder extension + hip flexor counter-stretch post-press. Lie face down, press up onto forearms or hands, keep pelvis down.
Friday · Gym

Upper B

Pull · Scapular control · Handstand drills
Phase 1, Weeks 1–2: Reconnect. Don't grind to failure.
Warm-Up · 10 min
Dead hang (passive)
3 × 20–30 sec
Decompress spine + shoulder traction. Let everything hang. Breathe.
Scapular pull-ups
3 × 8
No elbow bend — just depress and retract scapulae. Isolates the exact movement needed for handstands.
Band pull-aparts
15 reps
Activates rear delts and mid-traps. Hold band at shoulder height, pull apart to T-shape.
Downward dog walk-outs + pedal
5 reps
Your yoga warm-up in gym context. Walk hands out from standing, pedal heels.
Pull-Up Progression 🎯
Grease the Groove Protocol
5 sets · Stop 2 reps short
If your max is 6, do 4 per set. Rest 90 sec between sets. Every week all 5 sets feel easy → add 1 rep across all sets. Week 1: ~4 reps. Goal: 5 × 8 = 10 rep max within reach.
Slow Eccentric Negative Pull-Up
3 × 4–5 reps · 5 sec down
Jump or use box to get chin over bar. Lower as slowly as possible — aim for 5 full seconds. This builds the strength exactly where you fail on rep 7–8.

Handstand Drill Block · 10–15 min
Wall handstand hold (chest to wall)
3 × 20–30 sec
Chest facing wall is safer alignment than back to wall — forces proper hollow body. Build time here before going freestanding.
Handstand kick-up practice
5–8 attempts
Back to wall. Focus on one leg at a time, strong hollow body. Don't banana — keep ribs down. Kick-up is a skill: volume of attempts matters more than duration.
Pike walk-ins (feet up wall)
5 reps
From downward dog, walk feet up wall until hips are over shoulders. Builds compression strength and gets you comfortable being inverted.
Tuck compression holds
3 × 15 sec
Seated, pull knees to chest with a straight back. This is your straddle prep — the hip flexor compression that eventually lets you press to handstand.
Wednesday · Gym

Lower A

Squat · Hip hinge · Compression strength
Phase 1: Full range of motion. Let the hip flexor stretch happen in every rep.
Warm-Up · 10 min
Hip 90-90 with active holds
5 each side
The single best hip flexor and external rotator opener. Sit with both legs at 90°, rotate between sides. Hold each position actively — don't just collapse into it.
Lizard lunge with rotation
5 each side
Deep hip flexor and groin opening. Step foot outside hand, lower back knee. Add thoracic rotation — reach top hand to ceiling. This is your low lunge, loaded.
Leg swings
10 each direction
Forward-back then lateral. Hold wall for balance. Hip joint warm-up — don't force range, let momentum do it.
Glute bridge activation
15 reps
Wakes up glutes before squatting. Your hip flexors tend to dominate — this corrects that before you load.
Main Work
Compression Block · 10 min
L-sit hold
3 × 10–20 sec
THE compression exercise. Use parallel bars or floor blocks. Legs straight, hips at 90°, press down through hands. Direct training for Crow, Firefly, handstand.
Lying leg raise
3 × 12
Slow and controlled. Lower back stays pressed to floor the entire time. Lower abs + hip flexor — the same muscles that hold your legs up in a handstand.
Seated forward fold — active pulling
3 × 20 sec
Hands on feet or shins, actively pull yourself down rather than just folding. This is active hamstring flexibility — more effective than passive stretching long-term.

Tuesday · Home · Mat

Yoga + Drills A

Hip flexors · Hamstrings · 45 min
No class needed — this IS the session. Work slowly. Breathe into resistance.
Part 1 — Hip Flexor Release & Strengthening · 15 min
PNF Low Lunge (contract-relax)
5 reps × 10 sec each side
Tighten hip flexor for 10 sec (push front foot into floor without moving it), then actively relax deeper. PNF is the most effective method for real hip flexor change.
Psoas March — standing
3 × 12 each side
Standing, drive one knee up against light resistance or your own hand. Strengthens the hip flexor actively. You need length AND strength — passive stretching alone won't fix this.
Crescent lunge — lift back knee, hold 5 sec
5 reps each side
Back knee on mat in low lunge, then lift it off and hold. Loads the hip flexor stretch while demanding active control — harder than it sounds.
Reclined hero pose (Supta Virasana)
2 min hold
Deep passive hip flexor and quad release. Use a bolster or folded blanket under your back if knees complain. Don't force this one — it will come.
Couch stretch against wall
90 sec each side
Back shin up the wall, front foot forward in a lunge. One of the best psoas and rectus femoris openers. Hold passively, then add a gentle posterior pelvic tilt.
Part 2 — Hamstring Lengthening · 15 min
Active straight-leg raise
10 each side
Lying down, actively lift one straight leg as high as possible without the other leg shifting. Tests and builds active hamstring flexibility — more useful than static stretching.
Standing forward fold — slowly straighten knees
5 reps × 5 breaths
Start bent, hang heavy, then slowly straighten legs a little more each exhale. Teaches the nervous system to release the hamstring gradually rather than bracing against the stretch.
Strap-assisted passive hold
2 × 90 sec each side
Lying on back, strap around one foot, leg toward ceiling. Completely relax, breathe. Long passive holds are where genuine length changes happen — don't rush this.
Seated straddle — walk hands out
2 min hold
Legs wide, slowly walk hands forward, keep spine long. Adductor and hamstring combined. Breathe and let gravity work. This unlocks seated compression poses over time.
Pyramid pose (Parsvottanasana) with blocks
90 sec each side
Hands on blocks, front leg straight, fold over it. Very specific hamstring stretch — effective for anyone who carries tension in hips and lower back.
Thursday · Home · Mat

Yoga + Drills B

Inversion prep · Compression · 30–45 min
This is skill training, not stretching. Keep it focused and intentional.
Inversion & Compression Prep
Crow pose (Bakasana)
5–8 attempts × 10–20 sec
Core compression + wrist load + balance. Knees on upper arms (not in armpits), round the back, look forward. Your current arm balance to develop — more time here = faster progress.
Tripod or supported headstand
2–3 × 20–30 sec
Build hold time before attempting freestanding. Tripod: hands and crown of head form triangle. Supported: use wall. Focus on stacking hips over shoulders.
Straddle compression — flat back forward lean
3 × 20 sec
Legs wide, lean torso forward with a completely flat back. This builds the hip flexor strength needed for straddle handstand entry — not just flexibility.
Hollow body rock
3 × 20 sec
Ribs down, lower back pressed to floor, arms overhead, legs just off ground. Rock gently. Same body shape as a good handstand — your gym compression work and yoga come together here.
Handstand kick-up practice against wall
5–8 attempts
If space allows. Keep it playful — focus on alignment not duration. Each attempt is a rep. The handstand is a skill, and skills need volume of practice.
Child's pose + shoulder stretch
2 min
Cool down. Passive wrist and shoulder release. Thread one arm under for a shoulder stretch each side.
